Lab 2 - Project Team Formation

In this lab, we will be setting up the project teams for this semester. To complete this lab, you should go through all of the steps below as instructed and complete the associated activities. Upload the completed files to Canvas by the due date. When you are finished with the lab, show your work to your TA.

Est. Time ⏱
150 minutes

Meet Your Team

(20 Minutes)

1. Find your group members
Gather everyone on your team and arrange yourselves in a small circle so that everyone can see and hear all group members.

2. Assign a SCRIBE
You should pick one person to document today's lab in a MS Word doc your Google doc. You will show your documentation to your TA at the end of lab AND your team will upload a single PDF to Canvas at the end of lab time. Make sure everyone's name is on this document.

3. Introductions
Go around in a circle, state your name, home town, major, year in school, and where in the world you would like to go for a three-week "dream" vacation after the end of this semester (this is hypothetical so pick anything).
After all team members have introduced themselves, each team member should go around in a circle and state the names of the other team members. It is okay to ask your team members for help.
The team scribe should document everyone's name.

4. Schedule a meeting time
As a team, decide on a weekly meeting time and place to work on your group project.
  • You MUST pick a time outside of class/recitation.
  • All team members MUST be able to attend the meeting. Pick a time that works for EVERYONE.
  • You MUST meet for two hours each week. This can be one 2-hour meeting or two 1-hour meetings.
  • Think about where you might meet. You don't have to decide this right now, but come up with some options that work for everyone.
The team scribe should document your weekly meeting time.

Brainstorming

(30 Minutes)

1. Team name
As a team, decide on a team name. This can be anything but must be appropriate (i.e., no profanity, etc.). The team scribe should write down your team name.

2. Solicit ideas for your group project
As a team, you must decide what kind of software application you want to build. Each team member, should come up with at least ONE application idea or concept.
Next, go around in a circle and discuss each of the ideas you came up with. Then, choose your TOP FOUR ideas. You do not need to rank these, but your team must come to a consensus on which ideas are the top four.
Document the TOP FOUR ideas.

3. Application architecture
For EACH of your top FOUR ideas, document the basic application architecture that you might use to implement the idea. This should include front-end and back-end.
Examples: a website hosted on your PC, a website hosted in the cloud and available to the public, a Raspberry Pi data collector, a mobile application that connects to a cloud service, etc.
Don't forget to write down each applicaton architecture

Note
Today's decisions are NOT locked in stone. You have another week or so to decide your final application idea and team name.

A Fun Group Exercise

(45 Minutes)

Go here to complete a little brain teaser exercise designed to get you all working together.
You must talk to each other, listen to each other, and answer each of the questions in the exercise.
Document your answers (numbered 1 - 20). You will turn these in.

Turning Stuff in

(10 Minutes)

1. Create a single PDF
Create a single PDF containing the following:
  • Your team's GROUP NUMBER (ask your TA)
  • The first and last name of all of your team members
  • Your team name
  • The scheduled time for your weekly team meetings
  • Your TOP FOUR application ideas or concepts, including a brief description of the architecture and platform for each idea
  • Your team's answers to the exercise questions, numbered 1 - 20.
2. Submit a single PDF document to Canvas
One member of your team should submit your team's PDF to Canvas.
